About

This website is all about respecting people with low vision, cognitive, language, and learning disabilities by creating and designing accessible content.  Even with "perfect" font families in place it still may be hard to process the text.  Remember, less is more when it comes to accessible typography.  By choosing a common font and by limiting the number of fonts on your website you are on the way to making your typography accessible.  I love typography and this website is all about us learning together how to show our love for typography by learning how to use in a way that is fully accessible for everybody!

Studies have shown that common fonts most often win when reading speed and user preference of different fonts is compared. Something that I learned a few weeks ago is that screen readers often ignore styling methods, such as bold and italics, making these styles useless for blind users.
